02 Creative Brief

The Strategic Foundation

Brand Essence

The original rule-breaker of tennis. Prince has always existed to prove that the unconventional way is the better way — from the oversized racket that changed the sport to the players who refused to play by the establishment's rules.

Target Audience

18–35 year-old style-conscious players and tennis culture enthusiasts who see tennis not as a country-club sport but as a form of self-expression. They discovered tennis through culture (fashion, Netflix, social media) as much as through competition.

Key Insight

A new generation is falling in love with tennis — but they don't see themselves in the sport's traditional, buttoned-up identity. They want to play on their own terms, and they're looking for a brand that gives them permission to do that.

Brand Tension

Tennis has always had rules — about how to dress, how to act, who belongs. Prince was born to break those rules, but the brand lost its voice. Now the culture is finally catching up to what Prince always believed.

Single-Minded Proposition

Prince is the tennis brand for people who play the game their own way.

Reasons to Believe

  • Invented the oversized racket — mocked, then universally adopted
  • Phantom & Ripstick lines: engineered for feel and spin, the weapons of creative players
  • Heritage of backing unconventional champions (Rafter, Capriati)
  • DTC-forward model that connects directly with the player community

Tone & Manner

Confident but never arrogant. Culturally fluent. A little irreverent. Speaks to players as insiders, not consumers. More downtown cool than country club.

Mandatories

  • "Unconventional by Nature" tagline integration
  • Hero the Phantom and Ripstick racket lines
  • Drive DTC traffic (princetennnis.com)
  • Establish cultural credibility beyond the court
03 Creative POV

The Wrong Way Won

The strategic and cultural opportunity for Prince Tennis

They said the racket was too big. They said the grip was wrong. They said tennis belonged to the quiet and the polished. Prince didn't listen. We built the racket they laughed at — and it changed the game forever. Now a new generation is picking up the sport, and they're doing it all wrong too. Wrong clothes. Wrong attitude. Wrong side of the tracks. We say: perfect. Because in tennis, as in life, the wrong way has a funny habit of becoming the only way. Prince. Unconventional by Nature.

Cultural Context

Tennis is having its biggest cultural moment in decades. Tenniscore fashion, Break Point drama, and a new wave of charismatic players are bringing the sport to audiences who never cared before.

Strategic Opportunity

The legacy brands (Wilson, Head, Babolat) are doubling down on performance and tradition. No one is speaking to the cultural player — the one who sees a racket as a style statement as much as a weapon.

Brand Role in Culture

Prince is the gateway brand for tennis outsiders. We don't ask you to earn your place in the sport — we hand you a racket and tell you to play your game, your way.
